Did You Know?

Woody fragrances have a deep, timeless charm rooted in ancient traditions, with civilizations like Egypt, India, and those in the Middle East using woods such as cedar, sandalwood, and oud in rituals and daily life. These scents symbolized purity, spirituality, and even social status—oud, for example, was so rare it was called “liquid gold.” As perfumery evolved in Europe, especially from the Renaissance onward, woody notes like vetiver and patchouli became key in grounding and adding depth to fragrances, often forming the base that supports lighter top notes. Today, woody scents are celebrated in both men’s and women’s perfumes, known for their warm, earthy richness and lasting presence—conveying confidence, elegance, and mystery in every wear.
